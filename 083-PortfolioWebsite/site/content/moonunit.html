LIke many people, I moved my scant online social activities over to Bluesky a while ago, and have found the platform pretty interesting.

Not so much the social network aspect of it, but the underlying architecture and protocol, which empowers a user to own their own part of the network under their own domain. More importantly, thereby to own and control their own data.

I host my own Bluesky PDS under the colchester.social domain. Among other things, I set to knocking up a couple of Bluesky bots written in Python, for example Moon Unit. This basically just tells you all about what the moon is up to, with regards to phases and illumination (with help from PyEphem) and bits and bob of trivia.

The Python source code is, of course, all on GitHub, along with much of my other junk.

I've chosen to feature Moon Unit on this site, but other bots are in the offing. For example there's tide.colchester.social, which tells you all about what's happening with the tidal bits of the Colne, here in Colchester.

I mean, I live on the riverside, so could conceivably just look out the window at what the river and the moon are doing, but hopefully some of it all is useful to someone, and these have proven to be fun projects to work on.
